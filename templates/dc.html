Fun DC Facts

  1. Batman's No-Kill Policy: Unlike many other superheroes, Batman has a strict policy against killing his enemies. This moral code has been a defining characteristic of the Dark Knight and adds a layer of complexity to his character, as he navigates the line between justice and revenge.
  2. Wonder Woman's Creator: Wonder Woman was created by psychologist and writer William Moulton Marston. He envisioned her as a feminist icon, and her early stories often featured themes of female empowerment. Marston also played a key role in the development of the polygraph (lie detector).
  3. The Multiverse Concept: DC Comics introduced the concept of a multiverse, a vast array of parallel universes with alternate versions of characters, in the 1960s. This idea allowed for creative storytelling by exploring different iterations of beloved characters and has become a significant aspect of the DC Universe.
  4. Superman's First Appearance: Superman, one of the most iconic superheroes, made his first appearance in "Action Comics" #1 in 1938. Created by Jerry Siegel and Joe Shuster, Superman became the archetype for the superhero genre and laid the foundation for the entire superhero comic book industry.
